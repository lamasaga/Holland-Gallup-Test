竞争 / Competition（影响力 / Influencing）

标准定义 / Standard Definition：“竞争”才干主题突出的人通过与他人比较来衡量自己的进步。他们渴望获胜，享受竞争带来的刺激。
People exceptionally talented in the Competition theme measure their progress against the performance of others. They strive to win first place and revel in contests.

特征 / Feature：以他人的表现作为衡量自己进步的标准。他们力争第一，沉醉于竞赛之中。
measure their progress against the performance of others. They strive to win first place and revel in contests.

应用 / Application：你会为成功建立每日、每月和每年的指标，用来追踪自己的表现分数。这为团队注入能量，推动他们达到更高的成就。 你超越他人的动力可以激发创新和创造力。你天生善于把握衡量成就的现实指标，能够清晰地描述卓越表现是什么样子。 你获胜的动力以及对明确成功指标的需求，能够激励他人，让他们愿意加入你的团队。
You create daily, monthly and yearly metrics for success to track your performance scores. This creates an energy for your team that pushes them to reach higher levels of achievement. Your drive to outperform others can stimulate innovation and creativity. You are naturally attuned to real-world measures that assess achievement, leading you to describe what outstanding performance looks like. Your drive to win and your need for clear metrics that define success can inspire others and cause them to want to be on your team.

盲点 / Blind Spots：如果你是团队领导，和团队成员竞争只会适得其反。相反，找到方法用“竞争”才干来定义并庆祝团队的胜利。 你的“竞争”才干推动你，也可能让你推动别人——有时力度过大。记住，不惜一切代价的胜利不是胜利，而是自我挫败。在鞭策自己和团队取胜时，要确保自己的正直品格不受影响。 不要让竞争精神影响你与团队成员和同事的关系。找到每个人的强项，把他们放在最能发挥作用的位置，形成获胜的组合。 不是每个人都有你这样的求胜动力，不断把自己的表现和别人比较，可能会冒犯或伤害他们。记住，取胜不能以牺牲原则为代价。
If you lead a team, competing to win against your team is counter-productive. Instead, find a way to use your Competition to define and celebrate team wins. Your Competition talents push you, so you might tend to push others — sometimes too hard. Remember that winning at all costs isn't winning; it's defeating yourself. Make sure your integrity remains intact when you push yourself and your team to victory. Don't let your competitive spirit cloud your relationships with your team members and peers. Find ways to unite your team members by finding what each does best and positioning them for maximum effectiveness to create a winning combination. Be careful not to let your competitive spirit get in the way of your relationships. Not everyone has your drive to win, and constantly comparing your performance to theirs might offend them or hurt their feelings. Winning at all costs isn’t winning; it’s self-defeating. Make sure you maintain your principles when you push yourself toward ultimate victory.

排难 / Restorative（执行力 / Executing）

标准定义 / Standard Definition：“排难”才干主题突出的人是解决问题的行家里手。他们善于发现问题并从根本上加以解决。
People exceptionally talented in the Restorative theme are adept at dealing with problems. They are good at figuring out what is wrong and resolving it.

特征 / Feature：他们善于处理问题。他们能够找出问题所在并加以解决。
are adept at dealing with problems. They are good at figuring out what is wrong and resolving it.

应用 / Application：你有一种独特的能力，能够透过表面症状看到问题的根本原因——你的解决方案不是权宜之计，而是旨在持久有效。你是故障排除者，在这一角色中，你提高效率、更快达成目标。你清醒的分析使你在危机中极具价值。你收尾闭环、恢复秩序、清理混乱，并且享受这个过程。你的“排难”才干非常契合持续改进流程：你发现问题、确定如何修复，并把事情提升到新的水平。
You have a unique ability to look past surface-level symptoms to find the root causes of problems — your solutions are not stopgap, but rather are intended to last. You are a troubleshooter, and in that role, you increase efficiency and reach the goal sooner. Your clear-eyed analysis makes you extremely valuable in crises. You close the loop, restore order and clean up the mess — and enjoy the process. Your Restorative talents lend themselves well to continuous improvement processes. You find what is wrong, determine how it can be fixed and take things to the next level.

盲点 / Blind Spots：你对发现问题的关注，可能让你给人留下消极的印象——尤其是当你发现别人尚未考虑甚至尚未看到的问题时。帮助团队成员明白，你不是想“泼冷水”，而是在预见和避免那些会阻碍事情顺利进行的问题。 你通过研究问题和深入棘手复杂的情境获得能量，甚至可能在问题解决后感到一丝失落。因为这给你带来能量，要注意你可能会产生一种需求：在没有问题的地方制造问题来解决。根据你“排难”才干的性质，你可能关注流程而非人。该表扬时就表扬，不要过分关注缺点——无论是你的还是别人的。 你可能倾向于在别人遇到问题时冲进去救他们。要明辨情况，抵制替别人解决问题的冲动。更多时候，我们从自己面对和解决的问题中学到最多。别人可能认为你只看到他们的缺点和不足。记住，有时人们需要你看他们的成功并给予表扬。你倾向于关注流程而非人，可能会自动冲上去解决每个问题。有时别人需要自己解决问题，所以要给人们空间去实验和找到自己的解决方案。
Your focus on finding problems could bring you a reputation for being negative — especially when you find problems that others haven't considered, let alone seen. Help your team members realise you aren't wanting to "rain on their parade," but rather anticipate and avoid problems that would prevent the parade from even occurring. You are energised by examining problems and delving into thorny, complex situations. You may even feel a bit let down when the problem is solved. Because this gives you energy, be aware that you might feel a need to create problems to solve where none actually exist. Depending on the nature of your Restorative talents, you may focus on processes and not people. Give praise where it is due, and don't overly focus on shortcomings — yours or others'. You may tend to jump in and rescue others when they are dealing with a problem. Be discerning, and resist the urge to solve others' problems for them. More often than not, we learn the most from the problems we face and solve ourselves. Others might think that all you see are their flaws and shortcomings. Remember that there are times when people need you to see their successes and give them praise. You tend to focus on processes, not people, and you might automatically rush to solve every problem. Sometimes others need to solve their own problems, so try to give people space to experiment and find their own solutions.

回顾 / Context（战略思维 / Strategic Thinking）

标准定义 / Standard Definition：“回顾”才干主题突出的人喜欢追溯从前。 他们通过思考过去来了解现状。
People exceptionally talented in the Context theme enjoy thinking about the past. They understand the present by researching its history.

特征 / Feature：具有回顾主题的人喜欢追溯过去，通过对过去的揣摩来理解现在。
People with strong Context talents enjoy looking back. They understand the present by examining the past.

应用 / Application：定期回顾自己的工作和生活表现，反思过去的经验和成果，了解自己的优点和改进的领域。 识别自己在工作和生活中取得的成功和优势、技能和特长，认识到自己的价值和潜力。 利用回顾的机会来识别自己的学习需求和发展机会，不断提升自己的技能和知识。 根据自己的反思和目标，制定相应的行动计划，并持续跟踪和评估自己的进展。
Regularly review your work and life performance. Reflect on past experiences and results to understand your strengths and areas for improvement. Identify your successes and strengths — your skills and talents — in work and life, recognising your value and potential. Use opportunities to look back to identify your learning needs and development opportunities, continuously improving your skills and knowledge. Based on your reflection and goals, develop corresponding action plans, and continuously track and evaluate your progress.

盲点 / Blind Spots：· 苛求自己，对自己的行为和决策持过度批判的态度。这可能导致自我评价过低和自责心理的产生。 · 关注过去的错误和失败，而忽视了自身的成长和进步。他们可能无法看到自己取得的进步和积极变化。 · 回顾自己的过程中缺乏客观观察，过分依赖主观的情绪和偏见。这可能影响对自身行为的准确评估和深入理 解。
You may be overly critical of yourself and your behaviours and decisions. This can lead to low self-evaluation and feelings of guilt. You may focus on past mistakes and failures while overlooking your growth and progress. You may be unable to see the progress and positive changes you have made. In the process of reflection, you may lack objective observation and rely too much on subjective emotions and biases. This can affect the accurate assessment and deeper understanding of your own behaviour.

分析 / Analytical（战略思维 / Strategic Thinking）

标准定义 / Standard Definition：分析能力强的人喜欢探究事物的来龙去脉。 他们有能力思考可能影响局面的诸多因素。
People exceptionally talented in the Analytical theme search for reasons and causes. They have the ability to think about all of the factors that might affect a situation.

特征 / Feature：具有强大分析能力的人喜欢深入探究事物的来龙去脉，能够综合考虑各种因素对局势的影响。
People with strong Analytical talents enjoy exploring the reasons behind things. They are able to consider many factors that affect a situation.

应用 / Application：积极收集相关的信息和数据，利用各种资源，包括书籍、报告、研究和互联网，收集全面的信息。 运用逻辑思维和分析能力，对收集到的数据进行分析和解读，从中获取有价值的见解。 基于分析的结果，提出假设和推理，推导出合理的结论和预测。 在做出决策之前，评估潜在的风险和机会，制定相应的应对策略。 根据分析的结果，制定决策和行动计划。将决策细化为具体的步骤和时间表，确保执行的可行性和有效 性。 实施决策后，密切监测执行的结果 ,定期评估决策的效果，并根据反馈进行调整和改进。
Actively collect relevant information and data. Use a variety of resources, including books, reports, research, and the internet, to gather comprehensive information. Use logical thinking and analytical skills to analyse and interpret the data you collect, deriving valuable insights from it. Based on your analysis, put forward hypotheses and inferences, deriving reasonable conclusions and predictions. Before making decisions, assess potential risks and opportunities and develop corresponding response strategies. Based on your analysis, make decisions and action plans. Break decisions down into concrete steps and timelines to ensure feasible and effective execution. After implementation, closely monitor the results and regularly evaluate the effectiveness of your decisions, making adjustments and improvements based on feedback.

盲点 / Blind Spots：· 选择性地采集和使用数据，忽视或排除了其他重要的信息和数据源。 · 基于错误或不准确的假设进行分析，导致结论的不准确性和误导性。 · 只关注单一的因素或变量，忽略了问题的复杂性和与其他因素的相互影响。
You may selectively collect and use data, ignoring or excluding other important information and data sources. You may base your analysis on incorrect or inaccurate assumptions, leading to inaccurate and misleading conclusions. You may focus on only a single factor or variable, ignoring the complexity of the problem and its interactions with other factors.

People exceptionally talented in the Developer theme recognize and cultivate the potential in others. They spot the signs of each small improvement and derive satisfaction from evidence of progress.

特征 / Feature：欣赏他人潜力并善于发现进步的人，喜悦于细微的成长，激励他人不断进步。
You appreciate others' potential, notice even small improvements, and find joy in every sign of growth as you encourage people to keep progressing.

应用 / Application：观察和评估身边的人，发现他们的优点、才能和潜力，注意他们的成就和特长。 与他人建立良好的沟通和合作关系，了解他们的目标和需求，帮助他们发展他们的潜力，并在实现目标 的过程中提供指导和建议。 通过认可和赞赏，激发他人的动力和自信心。表达对他们的信任，并鼓励他们尝试新的挑战和机会。 通过良好的领导实践和积极的影响力，发展自己的领导能力，成为他人的榜样和引导者。 与他人建立导师关系，分享经验和知识，帮助他们发展专业能力和职业发展。
Observe and evaluate the people around you to discover their strengths, talents, and potential. Notice their achievements and special abilities. Build good communication and working relationships with others. Understand their goals and needs, help them develop their potential, and offer guidance and suggestions as they work toward their objectives.
