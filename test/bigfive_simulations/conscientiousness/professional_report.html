审慎 / Deliberative（执行力 / Executing）

标准定义 / Standard Definition：“审慎”才干主题突出的人做每一个决定或选择时都慎之又慎。他们会预先设想可能遇到的各种困难。
People exceptionally talented in the Deliberative theme are best described by the serious care they take in making decisions or choices. They anticipate obstacles.

特征 / Feature：他们最贴切的描述是：在做决定或选择时极为认真谨慎。他们会预先设想障碍。
are best described by the serious care they take in making decisions or choices. They anticipate obstacles.

应用 / Application：你天生谨慎，做决定时不急于行事。因此，别人可以信赖你的明智判断和经过深思熟虑的推理，尤其是在艰难决策时。你能看到别人容易忽视的潜在问题和障碍，这使你成为一个受人尊敬的故障排除者和问题识别者——有助于避免工作流程中的混乱和延误。在与他人互动中，你更喜欢多听少说，在听到所有事实之前不轻易下判断。这赋予你可信度，让你以公正直率著称。你注重隐私，也尊重他人的隐私。你谨慎而敏感地处理敏感话题，这在领导团队时是一项宝贵的资产。
You are naturally cautious, and take your time making decisions. As such, people can count on your sound judgment and your well-thought out reasoning when making a hard decision. You see potential problems and roadblocks that others tend to miss. This makes you a respected resource as a troubleshooter and problem identifier — helping to avoid messy slow-downs in workflow. In your interactions with others, you prefer to do a lot of listening and withhold judgment until you've heard all the facts. This gives you a trustworthiness and a reputation as an impartial straight-talker. You are a private person, and respect the privacy of others. You handle sensitive topics with discretion and sensitivity, a valuable asset when leading a team.

盲点 / Blind Spots：“分析瘫痪”可能让你无法及时向前推进。给自己设定完成尽职调查的截止日期，并确保有足够时间反思，以免错过重要截止日期。你谨慎的天性和关注可能出错的倾向，可能让你给人留下消极的印象。要指出，你的“审慎”才干通过避开可能使项目脱轨的“地雷”，帮助项目取得成功。 如果你领导团队，你为回避错误而放慢速度的倾向，可能让团队成员怀疑你是否忘记了他们的请求。确保你与团队保持定期沟通，避免误解。“审慎”才干强的人往往比较内敛，不擅长表扬。记住，定期的表扬和认可能激发卓越表现，要找到真诚的方式表达对出色工作的欣赏。 因为你做决定前会花时间仔细思考，别人可能认为你健忘、迟钝或害怕行动。准备好解释你并非如此，你只是在考虑所有可能的下一步。你谨慎而严肃的人生观可能给人留下冷漠、私密、难以接近且不太表扬他人的印象。请牢记这一点，尤其当生命中重要的人需要你的认可和肯定时。
The "paralysis of analysis" could keep you from moving forward in a timely manner. Give yourself deadlines to complete your due diligence, and ensure you have ample time to reflect so that important deadlines are not missed. Your cautious nature and inclination to focus on what could go wrong could give you a reputation of being negative. Point out that your Deliberative talents help you establish the success of the project by avoiding the landmines that could derail it. If you lead a team, your tendency to slow down in order to avoid mistakes may cause your team members to wonder if you've forgotten about their requests. Make sure that you communicate regularly with your team to avoid misunderstandings. Those with strong Deliberative talents often tend to be reserved and not prone to giving praise. Remember that regular praise and recognition fuels great performance, and find ways to genuinely show your appreciation for work well done. Because you take time to think carefully before making a decision, people might perceive you as forgetful, slow or afraid to act. Be prepared to explain that you are none of those things and that you are just considering all of the potential next steps. Your cautious and serious outlook on life might give the impression that you are a distant, private and unapproachable person who doesn’t give much praise. Keep this in mind, especially when the important people in your life need your approval and validation.

纪律 / Discipline（执行力 / Executing）

标准定义 / Standard Definition：纪律性强的人做事井然有序，有章有法。他们建立规程，并切实遵章守纪。
People exceptionally talented in the Discipline theme enjoy routine and structure. Their world is best described by the order they create.

特征 / Feature：他们喜欢常规和结构。他们的世界最好用他们所建立的秩序来形容。
enjoy routine and structure. Their world is best described by the order they create.

应用 / Application：你天生倾向于关注细节并创建结构，这使你能够以有条不紊、计划周全的方式处理任务。你能轻松地从杂乱和混沌中理出头绪、建立秩序，从而获得可以转化为更高效率的效率。你对细节的关注让你能够发现别人可能忽略的错误、疏漏和不一致，使你成为团队中处理潜在问题的宝贵资产。作为团队领导者，你能够为团队的工作习惯和项目执行带来结构、流程和秩序。
Your natural inclination to focus on the fine points and create structure enable you to approach tasks in an orderly and well-planned manner. You can easily sift through the clutter and chaos and create order, giving you an efficiency that can be leveraged for greater productivity. Your attention to detail enables you to see errors, mistakes and inconsistencies others might miss, making you a valuable asset to your team in addressing potential problems. As a team leader, you bring structure, process and order to your team's work habits and project execution.

盲点 / Blind Spots：你对坚持时间表的执着，可能让别人觉得你僵化或不够灵活。要理解，有时需要破例。如果你领导团队，应定期检查团队，看看需要做出哪些调整来改进团队流程。并非所有事情都可预测。当意外发生时，找到调整常规以应对挑战或机遇的方法。 你建立的框架能让项目顺利启动并运行。但要小心，框架不要变成牢笼；要对可能改进执行的新建议保持开放。由于你对可预测性和掌控感的需求，别人可能认为你僵化、不灵活。尽量不要自动否定那些不能直接套入你已有严谨框架的想法。就像你在结构和组织中茁壮成长一样，别人也需要创新的自由。要小心，不要期望别人完全遵循你建立的任何结构。
Your need to stick to a schedule may cause others to perceive you as rigid or inflexible. Understand that sometimes exceptions need to be made. If you lead a team, you should check in with your team periodically to see what adjustments may need to be made to improve the team's processes. Not everything that happens is predictable. When the unexpected happens, find a way to adjust your routine to address the challenge or opportunity unexpected occurrences bring. You develop frameworks that enable projects to get off the ground and run smoothly. Be careful that your framework does not become a prison; be open to new suggestions that might improve implementation. Because of your need for predictability and control, others might view you as rigid and inflexible. Try not to automatically dismiss ideas that do not directly fit into the neat framework you have created. As much as you thrive on structure and organisation, others thrive on the freedom to innovate. Be careful about expecting others to follow any structure you put in place.

成就 / Achiever（执行力 / Executing）

标准定义 / Standard Definition：“成就”才干主题突出的人工作努力，精力旺盛。他们乐于忙忙碌碌并有所作为。
People exceptionally talented in the Achiever theme work hard and possess a great deal of stamina. They take immense satisfaction in being busy and productive.

特征 / Feature：他们工作勤奋，精力十足，从忙碌和富有成效的状态中获得极大的满足感。
work hard and possess a great deal of stamina. They take immense satisfaction in being busy and productive.

应用 / Application：你天生具有强烈的干劲和驱动力，能够完成大量工作，并为团队设定节奏。你非常可靠——别人可以指望你完成任务、达成目标。把大项目拆分成可管理的小任务对你来说似乎轻而易举，因此你能帮助别人不再对大项工作望而生畏。你拥有持久的耐力，不仅喜欢努力工作，还能从努力工作中获得能量；你工作越努力，越发现自己拥有更多潜能。
You likely have an innate intensity and drive that enables you to get a lot done and set the pace for your team. You are dependable — others can count on you to get the job done and reach your goals. Breaking a large project down into manageable tasks likely comes naturally to you. As such, you can help others see big jobs as less daunting. You have stamina — not only do you like to work hard, you derive a lot of energy from working hard; the harder you work, the more capacity you find you have.

盲点 / Blind Spots：你的“成就”才干不断推动你，因此你可能也会推动别人——有时过于用力。因为你工作勤奋，你可能会对那些你认为不够努力的人感到沮丧；如此一来，同伴可能觉得你要求过高、不够灵活、爱评判他人。你对自己完成事情的能力充满信心，这可能使你在尚未充分了解项目的细节、微妙之处和需求之前就投入行动——这可能会给你带来麻烦。 由于你专注于完成任务，你可能会忽视或低估人和关系的价值。如果你是管理者或团队领导者，你可能需要提醒自己：管理不仅仅是通过人来完成工作，同样重要的是通过工作来成就人。“成就”才干突出的人可能会在别人不像他们那样努力工作时感到沮丧，别人也可能觉得他们要求太高。他们需要记住，并非每个人都对自己有如此高的期望，也并非每个人都像他们一样被驱使着努力工作。他们迫切想要完成事情的冲动，可能使他们在尚未了解全部情况时就接手项目或答应截止日期。在承诺之前，高“成就”才干者应确保自己有足够的时间和资源把事情做好。
Your Achiever talents push you, so you might tend to push others — sometimes too hard. Because you work hard, you may become frustrated with those whom you perceive as not working as hard as you do; as such, peers may see you as too demanding, inflexible and judgmental of others. Your confidence in your ability to get things done may cause you to jump in and get started on a project before understanding all the details, nuances and demands of the project -- and that can get you into trouble. With your focus on task completion, you might overlook or underestimate the value of people and relationships. If you are a manager or team leader, you may need to remind yourself that managing is not only about getting work done through people, but as importantly it's about getting people done through work. People with strong Achiever talents may get frustrated when others don’t work as hard as they do. Others may see them as too demanding. They need to remember that not everyone has the same high expectations for themselves or is driven to work as hard as they are. Their pressing need to get things done might cause them to take on projects or agree to deadlines before they know everything that’s involved. Before they commit to something, high Achievers should make sure they have the time and resources they need to do it right.

标准定义 / Standard Definition：统筹力强的人喜欢安排各种要素与资源。他们善于合理调配，以实现最大功效。
People exceptionally talented in the Arranger theme can organize, but they also have a flexibility that complements this ability. They like to determine how all of the pieces and resources can be arranged for maximum productivity.

特征 / Feature：他们喜欢确定如何安排所有要素与资源，以实现最大的生产力。
like to determine how they can arrange all of the pieces and resources for maximum productivity.

应用 / Application：你很可能天生就有能力同时管理多个项目和不同性格的人。你具备很强的多任务处理能力。你做事高效，能帮助团队采用简洁清晰的方式完成工作。你往往能清楚看到别人擅长什么、谁和谁配合默契，以及团队如何才能最好地达成目标。当环境变化时，你能迅速看清影响并相应调整计划。
You likely have an innate ability to manage multiple projects and personalities at once. You have great multitasking skills. You are efficient and can help your team employ concise, clear ways to get things done. You tend to clearly see what others can do well, who would work together well and how teams can best achieve their outcomes. When circumstances change, you quickly see the implications and can change plans accordingly.

盲点 / Blind Spots：你与生俱来的灵活天赋和组织风格，可能让别人难以看清背后的系统、结构和优先级。你会根据变化的情况或信息快速调整，这可能让其他人跟不上。记住初中代数课上的要求：展示你的过程。你不断调整和重新配置直到满意的倾向，可能让别人觉得你只是为了改变而改变。帮助他们理解重新配置的原因。 尽管你关于系统、空间和人员高效配置的直觉常常准确，但记得征求他人意见——他们可能看到你没有注意到的东西。你不断重新组织任务、项目和人员，可能会让其他人感到困惑。花时间解释你的方法以及它如何能带来更好的效果。“统筹”才干突出的人往往会对项目、流程和人员承担责任并视为己有。要注意，当你未经询问就接管控制权时，可能会激怒他人，或打击他们承担责任的积极性。
Your naturally flexible talent and organisational style can make it difficult for others to see the underlying system, structure and prioritisation. You make adjustment quickly based on the changing circumstances or information, and as such you can leave people behind. Remember the middle-school algebra directive: Show your work. Your tendency to configure and reconfigure until it's right can leave others wondering if you are making changes simply for the sake of making changes. Help them understand the reason for reconfiguring. Even though your intuition about configuring systems, spaces and people for maximum efficiency is often on target, remember to ask for input from others — they may see things that you don't. Your tendency to continually reorganise tasks, projects and people might confuse others. Take time to explain your approach and how it can work better. People with dominant Arranger talents tend to claim responsibility for and take personal ownership of projects, processes and people. Be aware that when you assume control without asking, you might irritate others or discourage them from taking responsibility.

专注 / Focus（执行力 / Executing）
